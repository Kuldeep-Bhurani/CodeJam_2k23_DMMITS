About ISRO

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is the space agency of the Government of India, headquartered in Bengaluru. It was founded in 1969 with the vision of harnessing space technology for national development.

ISRO has achieved numerous milestones, including launching satellites for communication, remote sensing, navigation, and scientific research. It is renowned for its cost-effective space missions and has made significant contributions to space exploration.

Chandrayaan Mission

Chandrayaan is India's lunar exploration program. The first mission, Chandrayaan-1, was launched in 2008 and was India's first mission to the Moon. It made significant discoveries, including the presence of water molecules on the lunar surface.

In 2019, ISRO launched Chandrayaan-2, a more ambitious mission with an orbiter, lander, and rover. While the lander Vikram had a hard landing, the orbiter continues to provide valuable data about the Moon.

Mangalyaan Mission

Mangalyaan, also known as the Mars Orbiter Mission, is India's first interplanetary mission. Launched in 2013, it made India the first Asian nation to reach Martian orbit and the first nation in the world to do so in its maiden attempt.

Mangalyaan was primarily designed to demonstrate India's capability to reach Mars and conduct scientific experiments. It continues to study the Martian surface and atmosphere, providing valuable data for research purposes.
