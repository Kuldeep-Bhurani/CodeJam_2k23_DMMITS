THE START

ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation) was founded by Dr. Vikram Sarabhai, an eminent Indian scientist and visionary, on August 15, 1969. Dr. Sarabhai was a pioneer in space research and played a crucial role in India's space program. The establishment of ISRO was driven by India's need for space research and satellite technology for the country's socio-economic development. Dr. Sarabhai firmly believed that space technology could address various challenges faced by India, such as communication, weather forecasting, resource management, and disaster monitoring. In the early stages, Dr. Sarabhai collaborated with a team of talented scientists, engineers, and experts from various fields. They worked collectively to lay the foundation of India's space program and build the necessary infrastructure to conduct space research and satellite launches. ISRO's first indigenous rocket, SLV-3 (Satellite Launch Vehicle-3), was developed under the leadership of Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, who later became the President of India. SLV-3's development was a significant milestone for India, as it marked the country's entry into space launch capabilities. The SLV-3 was a four-stage rocket designed to carry a payload of about 40 kilograms into low Earth orbit. On July 18, 1980, India achieved its first successful satellite launch using the SLV-3, placing the Rohini satellite into orbit. This achievement was a testament to the dedication and ingenuity of the Indian scientists and engineers who worked tirelessly to make India a space-faring nation. Since then, ISRO has continued to make remarkable progress, undertaking numerous successful missions, including lunar and interplanetary explorations, and becoming a globally respected space agency known for its cost-effective and reliable space missions. Driven by its motto of "Space technology for national development," ISRO continues to play a pivotal role in shaping India's space endeavors and contributing to the advancement of science and technology on a global scale.

Near Present

ISRO continues to explore new horizons and push the boundaries of space research, This time under Dr APJ Abdul Kalam. With its growing expertise and cutting-edge technology, ISRO has ambitious plans for the future. Some of the key areas that ISRO aims to focus on in the near future include: - Expanding satellite communication services to remote areas, enabling better connectivity and digital access - Advancing space research in the fields of astronomy, astrophysics, and planetary science - Launching more interplanetary missions to explore other celestial bodies in our solar system - Developing indigenous technologies for human spaceflight and conducting manned missions - Enhancing earth observation capabilities for environmental monitoring and disaster management - Exploring the potential of space-based solar power generation - Collaborating with international space agencies and organizations to foster global cooperation in space exploration Through its endeavors, ISRO aims to leverage space technology for the benefit of humanity and contribute to India's progress in various sectors, including agriculture, healthcare, and education. As the organization continues to evolve, it remains committed to its vision of making space accessible, affordable, and beneficial for all.
