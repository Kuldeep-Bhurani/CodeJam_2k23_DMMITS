Aditya L1 Mission

Launch Vehicle (PSLV)

Aditya L1 Solar Mission is expected to be launched aboard the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) from the Satish Dhawan Space Centre. PSLV is one of ISRO's reliable launch vehicles, and it has been utilized for various successful missions, including Chandrayaan and Mangalyaan. It will play a crucial role in placing Aditya L1 on its trajectory towards the Sun.

Overview

Aditya L1 Solar Mission, named after the Sun god Aditya, is a solar observation mission by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). The mission aims to study the Sun's outermost layer, the corona, and the chromosphere, along with the solar magnetic field. By studying the Sun, Aditya L1 will help in understanding the solar processes that impact space weather and influence our planet.

Scientists and Team

The Aditya L1 Solar Mission involves a dedicated team of scientists, engineers, and researchers from ISRO and various research institutions. Led by experienced mission directors, this team is committed to the success of the mission and advancing our knowledge of the Sun's behavior and its effects on our solar system.

Expected Mission Outcomes

Aditya L1's observations will provide crucial data to enhance our understanding of the Sun and its behavior. The mission aims to study solar phenomena like solar flares, solar wind, and magnetic fields. By studying these solar processes, scientists hope to gain insights into space weather and its impact on Earth and its technological systems. The mission is set to contribute significantly to the field of heliophysics and further our knowledge of our closest celestial neighbor, the Sun.
