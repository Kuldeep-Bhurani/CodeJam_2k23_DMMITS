Mangalyaan Mission

Launch Vehicle (PSLV)

Mangalyaan, also known as the Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM), was launched aboard the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) from the Satish Dhawan Space Centre on November 5, 2013. PSLV is one of the most reliable launch vehicles of ISRO and has been used for various successful missions, including Chandrayaan and other satellite deployments. It played a crucial role in placing Mangalyaan on its trajectory to Mars.

Overview

Mangalyaan, India's Mars Orbiter Mission, was launched with the objective of exploring Mars' surface, morphology, and mineralogy. The mission aimed to demonstrate India's space technology capabilities by reaching the Martian orbit. On September 24, 2014, Mangalyaan successfully entered Mars' orbit, making India the first Asian country to reach the Martian orbit and the fourth space agency in the world to do so.

Scientists and Team

Mangalyaan's mission involved a team of dedicated scientists, engineers, and researchers from ISRO and various institutions. The mission was led by Dr. Mylswamy Annadurai, who served as the Project Director and was instrumental in the success of the Mars Orbiter Mission.

Mission Achievements

Mangalyaan's successful entry into Mars' orbit marked a significant milestone for India's space exploration program. The mission achieved its primary objectives of capturing images of Mars, studying the Martian surface, and conducting scientific experiments. It provided valuable data on Martian atmosphere, methane presence, and other characteristics. Mangalyaan's success garnered international acclaim and strengthened India's position in space research and technology.
