Chandrayaan-3 Mission

Launch Vehicle (LVM3)

Chandrayaan-3 is expected to be launched aboard the Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle Mark III (GSLV Mk III), commonly known as LVM3. GSLV Mk III is ISRO's heavy-lift launch vehicle and has proven its capability with successful missions in the past. It is designed to carry heavy payloads to various orbits, including lunar transfer trajectory, and play a crucial role in India's space exploration endeavors.

Overview

Chandrayaan-3 is an upcoming lunar mission by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). Building upon the successes and learnings from previous missions, this mission aims to further explore the Moon's surface and conduct scientific investigations. It is a testament to India's commitment to space exploration and advancements in space technology.

Scientists and Team

Chandrayaan-3 involves a team of highly skilled scientists, engineers, and researchers who are working diligently to ensure the success of the mission. Led by experienced mission directors, this team is determined to achieve the objectives of the mission and contribute valuable data to our understanding of the Moon and its environment.

Expected Mission Outcomes

Chandrayaan-3 aims to enhance our understanding of the Moon, its geology, and its resources. The mission is expected to provide valuable data that could contribute to future lunar exploration and support further scientific research. By advancing our knowledge of the Moon, Chandrayaan-3 will pave the way for future space missions and foster international collaboration in space exploration.
