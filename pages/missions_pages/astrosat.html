ASTROSAT Mission

Launch Vehicle (PSLV)

Astrosat was launched aboard the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) from the Satish Dhawan Space Centre. PSLV is a reliable launch vehicle used by ISRO for a variety of missions, including placing Astrosat into its desired orbit for observing stars and galaxies in space.

Overview

Astrosat is India's first dedicated multi-wavelength space observatory. Launched on September 28, 2015, by ISRO, the satellite is designed to observe stars, galaxies, and other celestial objects in various wavelengths like X-rays, ultraviolet, and visible light. The mission has been instrumental in contributing to the study of the universe and expanding our knowledge of cosmic objects and phenomena.

Scientists and Team

The Astrosat mission involves a team of dedicated scientists, astronomers, and researchers from ISRO and various research institutions. These experts work tirelessly to plan and execute observations using the satellite's instruments and analyze the data received from Astrosat to further our understanding of the cosmos.

Mission Contributions

Astrosat has made significant contributions to the field of astronomy and astrophysics. It has facilitated the observation of distant galaxies, active galactic nuclei, star clusters, and other celestial objects in various wavelengths. The mission's data has provided valuable insights into the nature and behavior of cosmic sources and helped scientists gain a better understanding of the universe.
