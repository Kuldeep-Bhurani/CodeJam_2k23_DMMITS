Chandrayaan-1 Mission

Launch Vehicle

Chandrayaan-1 was launched aboard a Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV-C11) from the Satish Dhawan Space Centre. PSLV-C11 was a four-stage launch vehicle and successfully placed Chandrayaan-1 into the lunar transfer trajectory. The launch vehicle provided the necessary thrust to carry the spacecraft towards the Moon's orbit.

Overview

Chandrayaan-1 was India's first lunar probe, launched by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) on October 22, 2008. The mission aimed to study the Moon's surface and discover evidence of water molecules.

Scientists and Team

The Chandrayaan-1 mission involved a team of dedicated scientists, engineers, and researchers from ISRO and various institutions. Dr. Mylswamy Annadurai served as the Project Director and was instrumental in the success of the mission.

Mission Failure

Chandrayaan-1 successfully completed its mission objectives and provided valuable data about the Moon. However, on August 29, 2009, communication with the spacecraft was lost, and the mission was prematurely terminated after operating for more than 300 days. Despite the early failure, Chandrayaan-1 achieved several significant milestones and laid the foundation for India's future lunar exploration missions.
