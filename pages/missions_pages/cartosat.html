CARTOSAT Mission

Launch Vehicle (PSLV)

Cartosat missions are launched aboard the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) from the Satish Dhawan Space Centre. PSLV is one of ISRO's most reliable launch vehicles, known for its successful track record in deploying various satellites, including Earth observation satellites like Cartosat, into their intended orbits.

Overview

Cartosat series of satellites are dedicated Earth observation satellites developed by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). The primary objective of Cartosat missions is to provide high-resolution and detailed imagery of the Earth's surface, which is used for cartographic applications, urban planning, rural development, and infrastructure development projects.

Scientists and Team

The Cartosat missions involve a team of skilled scientists, engineers, and researchers from ISRO and other organizations. These experts work diligently to ensure the successful operation of the Cartosat satellites and the delivery of accurate and precise Earth observation data for various applications.

Mission Contributions

Cartosat satellites play a crucial role in supporting various developmental and planning activities in India and other countries. The high-resolution imagery provided by these satellites aids in resource management, urban planning, environmental monitoring, disaster management, and land-use mapping. The Cartosat series has significantly contributed to the field of cartography and geospatial applications, making it an essential asset for the nation's development and progress.
